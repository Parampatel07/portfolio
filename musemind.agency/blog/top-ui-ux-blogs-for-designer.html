Top UI UX Blogs For Designers To Follow For Inspiration

Post by
Nasir Uddin
Published
January 17, 2024
Catregory
UX Design
Read time
5 min

Whether you want to know about the latest design trends or review any tools or case studies of the complex UX process, UX blogs will have you covered. UX is focused on improving the digital world for the benefit of all people. This is a dynamic field that necessitates innovative methods. If you follow UX blog on a regular basis, you can improve your UX knowledge and knowledge of the industry as a whole.

In addition, numerous websites devoted to UI design will introduce you to case studies that detail the creation of a successful app or website and the steps used to increase its usability. Reading about similar cases can spark inspiration. In this regard, UX blogs can help guide your future initiatives' routes. In this blog,  just like the title says, I have gathered some top-quality resources to help you get enlightened about your desired topic! 

List of UI UX blog sites

Below is a curated list of 11 informative and insightful blog sites that cover a wide range of topics related to user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design. These blogs offer valuable resources, tips, and industry insights for professionals and enthusiasts in the field. 

1. Musemind

If you are a beginner and still in the process of getting the hang of the UX field, Musemind can help you with that. They have blogs that discuss every topic in-depth to understand so that you can implement them. You will also find case studies about their work to help you understand how to think and bring innovative design solutions!

2. UX Collective

The UX Collective is an independent design publication and blog encouraging designers to analyze their work critically. Hand-picked stories about user experience (UX), graphic design, and product design from around the web.

3. Nielsen Norman Group

When one of UX's founding fathers is in the blog's name, you know that it's going to be excellent. This UX blog was co-founded by Don Norman and featured research-driven educational posts. Their blogs are centered on user testing and user behavior. They also cover eCommerce user experience, mobile design, and usability.

4. Toptal

The Toptal Design Blog serves as a central location for in-depth design studies written by experienced designers. They discuss every plausible topic relating to design. You can find blogs here that cover topics such as user interface design, user experience design, brand design, mobile design, the design process, the life of a designer, tools, and tutorials.

5. Usability Geek

If you want to improve your work's quality, start reading blogs from them! Because just like the name, their blogs are mostly about how you can enhance the usability and aesthetics of your work. They also covered other topics in blogs as well.

6. UX Magazine

This website will give food for your thought! UX Magazine is a free community resource exploring all experience design aspects. They produce a steady stream of exciting and valuable content by collaborating with practitioners and industry leaders who are experts in all areas of user experience design.

7. Career Foundry

The CareerFoundry blog is for anyone considering a UX design career and those learning UX or just starting out. You'll find clear explanations of all the key principles, such as design thinking and human-centered design, and simple step-by-step guides to all aspects of UX design, from user research to your final prototyping.

8. Awwwards

The Awwwards blog may look UI-heavy, but it's also great for UX designers. You'll find articles and case studies to stoke your creative fire in the blog's UX/UI section, such as this post on inventive and unconventional e-commerce experiences exhibiting unique approaches to microscopy and website navigation. Visual examples and illustrations accompany every piece of content, bringing theory to life. Awwwards combines UX and UI design and how they work together.

9. Smashing Magazine

The Smashing Magazine blog is well-known among those who have dabbled in web design. If you want to improve your UX design workflow and the quality of the experiences you create, then you should check out Smashing Magazine. Experts in UX design, such as Nick Babich and Vitaly Friedman, regularly contribute to this site. Smashing Magazine talks about everything, from monthly web development to a monthly list of the most significant design changes.

‍

10. UX Planet

UX Planet bills itself as a "one-stop resource for everything related to the user experience." It has everything from guides for beginners to articles on thought leadership. If you're new to UX, go to the UX for beginners section. There, you'll find a lot of content to help you get started, such as a UX primer for people who aren't designers. 

The best feature of UX Planet is the diversity of its contributors' backgrounds and shared interests in UX. So, in addition to the standard how-tos and guidelines, you'll also discover words of wisdom, motivation, and a lot of "real talk" about what it's like to embark on a career in UX.

11. UX Matter

UX Matters is a UX blog that focuses on research, design, and how easy it is to use. It has UX tips for everyone, from new designers to those with a lot of experience and those just starting out in the field.

This UX blog has a lot of helpful advice for UX designers, such as how to stay on budget while doing research and how to talk to developers. It also has a lot of information on a wide range of interesting and important topics. You'll find posts about everything from how to make AI apps that people can use to reviews of new books in the field.

Blog sites, especially  for UI UX design tools 

XD Ideas - www.xd.adobe.com

XD Ideas is a fairly new blog in the UX blogosphere. It is powered by Adobe XD. The blog is divided into logical sections that make finding what you're looking for easy. There are helpful guides for learning the basics, key design concepts and principles, beginner-friendly career tips, and a lot of information on design processes, from wireframing to prototyping.

Inside Design (invisionapp)

You have probably heard of the prototyping tool Invision. They maintain an excellent blog site entitled Inside Design. If you're in the UX industry, the Inside Design blog can help you broaden your horizons, particularly if you're interested in optimizing UX processes and teams and gaining an understanding of product design and development. This is a great place to discover new UX trends, resources, tools, and events.

Justinmind‍

The Justinmind blog covers a wide range of issues related to user experience design, and the entries are both informative and entertaining. The Justinmind blog features informative articles on a wide range of topics related to user experience design. These range from how-to tutorials on our prototype tool to news on the current UX design trends.

UX Pin - www.uxpin.com

Aside from being a useful UX design and management tool, UXPin also generates high-quality blogs. Keep up with the newest standards by reading articles on subjects like enhancing your brand's UX style guide and optimizing your website's performance. Following the blogs listed here, you can keep up with the constantly evolving UX design market. 

Axure - www.axure.com

Axure is one of the popular prototyping tools. And they did a great job by building their blog site. This is an incredible blog site to help you level up with your design game. You will find incredible blogs that will help you make your designs and prototype faster and better. 

How to Become a UX Designer?

It's normal for anyone will be excited to be a UX designer, and why not it is one of the most in-demand careers. If you are skilled enough, earning six figures is quite easy.  But, you must first figure out if this is your true calling. Do you like designing? Do you think you are creative enough to pull this out? 

Read blogs, and watch YouTube videos to fully understand the potentiality of the field. Now let's assume you are fully assured you want to be UX field. Now, what next? Keep on reading the following steps to know:

Get Certified 

You can still be a UX designer without certificates, but being certified will increase your credibility!  A certification represents you have gone under proper training by professionals, have sat for exams, and passed all the requirements. 

You can choose a bachelor's degree, but if you have money and time constraints, you can complete any course from e-platforms, such as Course Era or Udemy; there are more niche platforms, like  CareerFoundary, and Google has also provided courses on UX professional. To know more about some good UX courses, read this part. You can enroll in UX courses at IT institutes near you if you need more offline training.  

Learn Multiple tools 

Limiting yourself to just one tool will reduce your chances of working in multiple companies. Plus, you won't be able to perform effectively. To unleash your creativity, learn all the necessary tools for UI/UX.  

Not everyone uses Figma or Adobe; there are more tools for wireframing, prototyping and illustrating. So, to increase your chances of working with dynamic companies, get your hands familiar with these tools. 

Create a Portfolio 

During the duration of a UX design course or Bootcamp, you will normally be required to finish at least one project. You should continue creating designs outside the course to gain as much experience with various tools and design types as possible.

When you are satisfied with your designs, you should think about sharing them on online platforms such as Behance and Dribble so that you can obtain visibility and receive feedback. This is a wonderful opportunity to enlarge your portfolio and network of contacts within the UX design community.

Furthermore, if you continue to share your work based on different industries such as fintech, health care, or sports, employers will understand you have a vast knowledge of that specific industry, increasing your chances of getting a job quickly.

Note: Keep updating your profile, and add the latest work you have completed. This will give your portfolio diversity. 

Do an Internship Under A Good Agency

First, get some hands-on experience. Instead of expecting higher pay, look for a job where you can learn the most. You can also put your skills to use through a design internship or freelance work. This will be a wonderful experience to add to your resume as well as an excellent method to broaden your network.

It will also help you develop strong project management, cooperation, and communication skills. These soft talents are just as vital as the technical skills required of a UX designer. Internships and freelance opportunities can be found on LinkedIn, Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer.com.

Develop meaningful networks 

Networking is essential for two reasons: it can assist you in finding mentors and jobs. If you want to become a UX designer, talking to designers who have been working in the field for a long time can teach you a lot about how to be successful. You can learn about the best tools for learning, new trends, and more. If you build a strong relationship with them, they may even tell you about job openings at their companies and give you a referral.

Enroll in Advanced Courses 

If you want to climb higher positions in UX fields, other than years of experience, you need more technological knowledge; it can be cognitive psychology, it can be human-technology, information architecture, UX research, front end development; the choices are endless. If you want to be a principal designer from a junior UX designer, you need more than just technical skills.  So Never stop learning! 

Before ending the blog, here's a suggestion for some books that will help you kick-start your career. You are welcome!
